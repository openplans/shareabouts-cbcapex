What is this map?

This project will provide an ongoing mechanism for collecting ideas and suggestions from the public on where they believe the City government ought to be spending and investing capital budget dollars. Public input is vital to ensuring that government spending is done in greater alignment with the priorities and values of our communities. Ultimately, this project is intended to be a tool for increasing citizen participation and supporting the democratization of the City’s budget process.

The Who, What and How of Posting

  • Anyone can post ideas, even if you don’t live in this Community Board District
  • Eligible ideas must be for capital projects: physical infrastructure for public benefit, such as park improvements or new technology for schools. Expense projects, such as afterschool programs or expanding bus service, are not eligible.
  • To add a spot, drag the map until the crosshairs are over the desired location. Ideas should be located within the Community Board District boundaries.

Here are some examples of eligible and ineligible ideas:

Roadway/ Streetscape

Eligible
  • Rehabilitation to area bridges
  • Pedestrian safety improvements at intersections
Non-Eligible
  • Repairs to privately-owned sidewalks

Building/ Facility

Eligible
  • Renovation of local community center
  • Acquisition and installation of back-up generators for public housing
Non-Eligible
  • Paying bills for electricity of a public building
  • After school programs

Park/ Open Space

Eligible
  • Green roofs on schools
  • Park rehabilitation and improvements
Non-Eligible
  • Educational programs
  • Planting a street tree
